About Anime & Manga

Manga (Japanese comics/graphic novels) and Anime (Japanese animation) are distinct yet interconnected entertainment forms. Manga are generally hand-drawn, printed stories, while anime are animated adaptations or original productions featuring vibrant visuals and voice acting. Both are major global cultural influencers offering diverse genres and detailed storytelling.

Manga

Comic

  • Animated animation (moving images).
    It involves high production value with voice acting, music, and sound effects, usually in color.
  • Production and Distribution
    Produced by studios with large teams, often acting as an adaptation of a successful manga, light novel, or game.
  • Medium and Format
    Printed, static still images. They are generally produced weekly or monthly in anthology magazines (on cheap paper) and later collected into volumes (tankōbon).
  • Art Style and Reading Order
    Generally in black and white (except for special editions). They use distinctive stylized drawings (large eyes, emotive faces) to convey emotion efficiently under tight deadlines. They are read from right to left.
  • Production and Distribution
    Typically serialised in large, cheap anthologies first, then compiled into volumes.
  • Medium and Format
    Printed, static still images. Western comics are often sold as monthly, higher-quality magazines.
  • Art Style and Reading Order
    Typically in full color. Art styles vary widely but tend to be more detailed or realistic than traditional manga. They are read from left to right.
  • Production and Distribution
    Often published as single monthly issues before being collected into graphic novels.
